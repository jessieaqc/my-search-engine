              Flawless Skin Starts with Makeup Removal:
              A Comprehensive Guide!

              What Is Makeup Remover?What Are the Benefits of Makeup Remover?Why You Should Never Sleep Before Removing Your MakeupExplore NIVEA’s Makeup Remover ProductsHow to Use The Makeup Remover Effectively?Frequently Asked Questions About Face Acne

              It may ring a bell when you create your usual look or a long-lasting makeup for a special day—and regardless of whether the result holds or smudges a bit, it isn’t easy to wash it off in the evening. And it's also not a good idea to go to bed with makeup residue on your skin, because the next morning you could end up with nasty little bumps of inflammation on your face.

              It's important to cleanse as thoroughly as possible – but what and when to use is the key. In this article, we'll show you the best way to use the most suitable makeup remover so that your face can breathe a sigh of relief while looking healthy and well-maintained!

              What Is Makeup Remover?

              Makeup removers have been carefully created to assist you in removing any kind of makeup. You can get rid of every trace of makeup–down to the last pore–by using the right solution. Some of them are perfect for cleaning up everyday makeup, while some formulas are perfect to remove cosmetics like waterproof mascaras, special effects makeup or body paint.

              Makeup removers have two categories: one is water-based, which means it is in a liquid form—you can use it with a cotton pad or with your bare hands. The other version is the wipe, which is a biodegradable textile already soaked with the liquid.

              What Are the Benefits of Makeup Remover?

              • You've probably done it before: standing in the shower or sitting in the bath, quickly washing your face with soap. Besides the fact that using soap on your body probably didn't do your complexion any good, you may have been very surprised of your reflection because your makeup didn't disappear, and it just smeared all over your face. A makeup remover makes it easy to clear up any residue.
              • As modern makeup lasts longer and longer and must withstand various environmental factors, it's even more difficult to clean your face afterward. With the right makeup remover, you can clean up any makeup without leaving a trace, no matter how long-lasting it promises to be.
              • If you don't thoroughly remove your makeup, your pores can quickly become clogged, causing blackheads and acne. Your complexion will be grateful if you use the right make-up remover for your skin type to prevent blemishes.
              • Throughout the day, your face also sheds natural oils as a cleanser, but in the meantime, a lot of micro-dirt and dust can get trapped in your foundation, and when they mix together, it can increase the risk of inflammation. You can remove all these residues with a complex makeup remover, while still giving yourself a gentle cleanse to prevent any inflammatory reactions.
              • In some extreme cases, your skin can become sensitive, causing an itchy, allergic reaction to certain makeup products. Using a makeup remover can not only prevent these unpleasant consequences, but also prepare your skin to use other skincare products effectively afterwards by cleansing it thoroughly.

              Why You Should Never Sleep Before Removing Your Makeup

              Your body regenerates while sleeping, not just your brain. It's no coincidence that not only the morning routine, but also the nightly routine before going to bed plays an important role in staying healthy. This includes proper facial cleansing, including thorough makeup removal. You may say that you don't have the energy to wash off your makeup now, but in the long run, you'll save yourself a lot of problems.

              Explore Face Cleansing Products

              When you wear makeup while you sleep, you hinder the skin's natural renewal process. Soon enough, the skin stops breathing and the first signs of fatigue appear. Powder-based products usually help keep the skin dry, but at night they can dry it out even more and mix with the oils released by the skin. This can cause the skin to flake and become even oilier in some areas, which can lead to combination skin, not to mention that this can increase the risk of developing inflamed acne.

              A lot of makeup focuses on the eyes, but the human eye is also one of the most important and sensitive parts of the body. During sleep, improperly removed makeup can irritate and itch the eyes, causing serious infections. Always remove waterproof mascara as best you can!

              Since most makeup is oily or mineral-based, it may clog pores overnight and causes more acne breakouts. Also, makeup residue and the ingredients it contains can cause various skin blemishes if left on all night, or even cause inflammation or allergic reactions that lead to developing a more sensitive skin. Therefore, it's important to remove your makeup before going to bed and moisturize your skin afterwards so that it can breathe.

              How to Use The Makeup Remover Effectively?

              • First of all, always wash your hands before touching your face or any cleansing product.
              • Take one (or two) makeup cotton pad and soak it with a small amount of water as a makeup remover. It should not be more than one to three drops.
              • Do not use abrasive motions, just sweep the cotton pad with gentle pressure.
              • Rinse your face with lukewarm water. Gently soak it up with a dry and soft paper towel.
              • Depending on your skin type, use other facial care products - toner, moisturizer, or other care products.

              How to Use Makeup Remover Wipes Effectively

              • Before touching any wipe, always wash your hands!
              • Take one to three wipes based on the amount of makeup on your skin, and gently rub them over your skin.
              • Always start on the inside and sweep outward: from your nose to your ears. For heavy makeup, circular motions are also recommended.
              • To remove eye makeup, close one eye and gently clean the area around the eye.
              • Always reseal the pack after removing makeup. This way, the wipes stay fresh and do not dry out.

              Frequently Asked Questions About Face Acne

              • Is it OK to sleep with makeup on once in a while? Though it may seem that it won’t hurt to sleep just once in makeup, even just one time can be enough to result in a long-lasting skin irritation or an infected styed eye, impairing your vision.
              • Can I just use face wash to remove makeup? Of course! This is a better solution than using a regular soap which can be harsh.
              • Is it OK to use makeup remover every day? Makeup remover can be used safely daily. You just need to find the one that suits your skin type.
              • Why use makeup remover wipes? Makeup remover wipes are an easy solution to clean your skin: just one swipe can effectively remove makeup residues and other surface debris while it also hydrates your skin.
              • Is micellar water good for your makeup? Micellar water is a type of makeup remover that helps to cleanse your skin and nourish it. The micelles do help to break down makeup on your skin, but if you have a heavier stage makeup or a body art, you should always use a stronger cleanser as well.
