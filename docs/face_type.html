Types of Face Shapes: How to Know Yours

Written by aesthetic content writer Ellie Watson. Reviewed by Medical Director Dr Ameera Laher

Ever wonder why a particular makeup look, facial style, or set of glasses suits one person so well but not another? Their face shape is typically the one that matters. Understanding the inherent structure of your face serves as a basis for smart style, grooming, and cosmetic decisions rather than just being a beauty technique.

Whether you want to improve your appearance or just understand your distinctive features, this guide will explain face shapes, how to identify yours, and why it all matters.

What are Facial Shapes?

Face shapes are your face’s overall dimensions and contours as seen from the front. Your chin, jawline, cheekbones, and forehead’s relative length and width are all included in this.  Your face’s shape is a geometric outline created by these components combined.

Being aware of the face shape gives you an advantage when it comes to skincare products and styling advice. It is helpful in determining the ideal facial contouring or in comprehending why some beauty trends work better for other people than for you.

Different Types of Face Shapes

Though each individual face is unique, there are seven widely recognised different face shapes. Here’s a breakdown:

 

  • Oval Face

The oval face is often regarded as the perfect face shape in both beauty and medical aesthetics since it is the most balanced and adaptable.  It has a little smaller chin, softly rounded features, and is longer than wide.

  • Round Face

Soft curves and few angles characterise this look, which is nearly as wide as it is long.  The jawline is often rounded, and the cheeks are full. Round face shapes women look gentle and youthful.

  • Square Face

The square face is characterised by its prominent shape, which includes a large forehead and a strong, angular jawline. It is about the same breadth from forehead to jaw and has few curves.

  • Rectangle Face

This facial shape resembles an oval but is longer, with an elongated chin and high forehead.  The width of the forehead, jawline, and cheekbones is close together. Both men and women with tall, attractive features often have it.

  • Heart-Shaped Face

This shape, which often features high cheekbones, is characterised by a thin, pointed chin and a broader forehead. It’s a particular face shape for women, giving them a naturally feminine look.

  • Diamond Face

This distinctive facial shape features big, well-defined cheekbones with a thin forehead and jaw. It stands out due to the sharp angles, which also give the face a contoured appearance.

  • Triangle Face

The triangle face is the opposite of the heart shape, with a narrower forehead and a wider jawline. Because of its unique shape, it needs to be styled carefully to balance the jaw’s width.

How to Determine Your Face Shape?

Wondering how to identify your face shape? Here’s a step-by-step method:

  1. Measure Your Forehead – Find the widest point and measure from one side to the other.
  2. Measure Your Cheekbones – Place the measuring tape across the cheekbones, just below your eyes.
  3. Measure Your Jawline – Start at the base of your ear and measure to the centre of your chin, then double it.
  4. Measure Your Face Length – Start from the centre of your hairline to the tip of your chin.

Does the length of your face exceed its width? Does your forehead narrow more than your jawline? These comparisons will assist you in determining which of the several facial shape kinds is your own.

Furthermore, certain apps and web resources use artificial intelligence (AI) to assess facial shapes, providing prompt findings based on facial proportions and symmetry.

Why Face Shape Matters?

Understanding face shapes is not just a vanity exercise — it serves practical purposes in multiple areas:

  • Cosmetic Enhancements

The contour of your face defines where volume should be enhanced or dipped, whether you’re thinking about fillers, Botox, or facial shaping. A person with a square face, for instance, could want to soften their jawline, whilst someone with an oval face might benefit from cheek contouring.

  • Makeup Application

Different face shapes require distinct techniques for contouring and highlighting. For example, creating a heart-shaped highlight on the temples offers balance, while contouring beneath the cheekbones for a round face enhances definition.

  • Facial Hair Grooming

Beard styles can significantly change how wide or narrow a man’s face appears. A goatee could look good on a square jaw, whereas a full beard might go well with a long face.

  • Skincare and Treatments

Understanding facial contours might even help customise cosmetic procedures like facials and peels.  Structural characteristics are often associated with areas that are prone to dryness or oiliness.

Final Thoughts

There isn’t a perfect face shape. Everybody has a different facial balance and bone structure, and beauty is about embracing and enhancing your inherent features. You may customise skincare, grooming, and cosmetics to meet your unique needs by being aware of the various facial shapes.

FAQ

Can I change my face shape?
Not permanently, but treatments like fillers, contouring, and even hairstyles can enhance or soften certain features.
What is the most common face shape?
Oval and round are among the most common, especially in face shapes for women.
Is there a difference between face shapes men and face shapes women?
Yes. Men face shapes often have more angular jaws and longer face lengths, while women face shapes tend to be softer and rounder.
What is the rarest face shape?
The diamond face shape is considered one of the rarest due to its narrow forehead and jawline combined with high cheekbones.
Does my face shape change with age?
Yes, bone density and fat distribution can shift over time, subtly changing your face shape.
Do certain face shapes age better?
Oval and heart face shapes often show signs of ageing more gradually due to balanced proportions.
Can facial exercises change your face shape?
While facial exercises may tone muscles and improve circulation, they don’t significantly alter bone structure or your fundamental face shape.
